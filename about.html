About

History

Early years

Around the fall of 2020, I got a tutor to teach me scratch while I was in lockdown. My first game was a tag game made in my old scratch account lucasm30. Later that day, I started working on my very first animation. It was a small cartoon of people dancing to the ducktales moon theme called "Master Dancer".

After my course finished, I haven't really done any coding for a while. Although, on after my friend introduced me to gumball, I was inspired to make my own animation. Which is how I came up with the idea of Renchwood during the late 2021. I was also interested in making stop motion animation. I made my first stop motion film called Piplin and then laker the crab slayer, a movie based on how my cousin who killed a crab with a stick 9 years ago(it later turned into a video game).

Redram

After taking heavy inspiration from Pixar, I started my own film studio titled "Redram". The studio was originally supposed to be producing several full length 1 hour long films such as:

  • Girfins Quest - I'll talk more on that later
  • Comedic Cowboys - Movie about a bunch of cowboys whose careers are chosen by the head of the town. With the problem being the cowboys wanting to be a comedian but cant due to the restrictions of their their career.
  • Tornado guy/germ guy - pretty obvious, a movie about this guy who gets stuck in a vacuum cleaner and gets germs and becomes a superhero. The other idea was a guy who can use wind to move objects around.
  • Laker the crab slayer - the original short film turned into a full length movie.
  • Grey's globe - goofy black and white film that takes place in the 1940s
  • Grey's globe 2 - goofy black and white film that takes place in the 2000s

The studio was later shut down after realizing that I would need 100s of animators and a million dollar budget to be able to make those films. Which meant all my films were cancelled. Although I do hope I can turn some of those ideas into a book or video game.

Messydesk is born

When I started 5th grade, I was learning about order of operations. Which made me want to write a song about the subject. I was thinking of the video being an animation on scratch. Although I haven't really used scratch for a while, so I had to create a new account. I named my account "Messydesk-studios" because of an animation I emailed to someone titled "Animation I made in my messy desk". Hence the name. I also chose the name because it represents a busy and cluttered mind. The PEMDAS animation was never finished(I didn't know how to make songs).

For a while, the messydesk account was used as just a spare account for small little scratch projects such as tag or 3d stuff. I had a friend known as Aboomination. We knew each other in our old school until he left, then we became best friends when I moved to the same school as him. We both discovered our passion for animating and I helped him work on an animation series called Trap Master. It was never finished but it helped develop my skills for animation with the creation of my own lip syncing system which would be used for many of my future animations.

The Lucas and Adam show

Around March of 2023, I was assigned to make a project about oil for my science assessment. Now you could make a poster or essay but you were allowed other media types such as videos. Me and Aboomination both agreed to make an animation. The animation was about oil and was finished in only 2 weeks. The animation was titled "The Lucas & Adam show - episode one: oil" which is considered my first true animation. Though the animation may not be as impressive to me now, it was revolutionary back then especially for a 10 year old me. The Lucas and Adam show is still one of the most important animations I've ever worked on. The animation won an award a year later for most outstanding project by an 8-11 year old.

Websites

My friend formed a group called the Sus Cringers and later the ROSC. Being in the group was a huge part of my friendships in middle school and also my web design skills. I made a website using google slides for the SC because google sites were too limited, I wanted to take inspiration from newgrounds and used some gradients. Eventually I got so good at designing websites that I planned to make a website of my own that was entirely inspired by 2000s websites called The Cheesestick society.

The Cheese Stick society was one of my favorite websites that I had the most fun making but was closed due to inactivity. I contributed to more slidesites such as Aboomistudio and Platsville chat. I eventually tried other platforms but it didn't work well for me. I made 9 websites for Platscape OS but scratch wasn't really built for websites. Nerd center was like the Cheesestick society except it looked more like a geocities website. Google sites simply were too basic and limited so I tried wix which looked perfect. I can make and publish websites that are actual websites rather than google slides presentations. Although wix was too cluttered, it was too complicated unlike the straight forward google slides that didn't have to ask for my business and location just to make a new website.

The Animator Battles

After months of short animations, me and Aboomistudio were planning on making our next big project. It would be the first episode of a series called the animator battles. The show was about 10 underrated animators who would compete against each other in a stadium to decide who is the best animator. Aboomination and I were planning for this project to be big. We made the characters fighting OC's of actual animators who signed up for the project and we wrote a script for 5 episodes. I even wrote a website for the series. The project was planned to be our biggest project. After 6 months of hard work, the animation was finally finished. Only a few of the people who signed up saw it and cared for the animation. It took Aboomination to be aware of it being finished since he only worked on a few scenes. Although it didn't matter because my family loved it! The Animator Battles may not be my best animation but it is one of my greatest achievements during my tween years. It had fast paced fight scenes, good quality visuals and it was 6 minutes long. I didn't care if it was popular or not, I was very proud of how much I've improved in my animation skills.

HTML and the golden age of ROSC

I made a web website called Platsville which was very impressive because unlike google slides, you could log in, post and message people. The website was used by a lot of my classmates which made wix the perfect web hosted service. Although it was limited just like google sites, I couldn't customize the layout to make it look more retro. I was only limited to changing the colors, fonts and border radius etc. Another thing was that it was quite slow and oftentimes only some profiles would load properly.

After seeing the huge flaws in different web hosting services, I decided to use neocities. I've been trying to use neocities for a while but I ditched it easily because I didn't know a lot of html. After reading a book my dad gave me about coding, I properly learned html and tested my skills by making simple tables, divs and webpages. Soon I was fully ready to make a html website all by myself and made a recreation of the Aboomistudio website.

Eventually got good enough to the point where I made a full ROSC website with news, directories and a chatroom. The ROSC was now in its golden age after having a fully functional website you could view in your browser. People started asking me to make websites for them such as JSS(which no one used).

During summer break, I finished all of my unfinished games I made during 2024. This includes Save Your Fries, Trench Gunner, Swiftie Shooter and Zbank. The Middle School Musical

Remember when I mentioned how The Animator Battles was my most impressive but not best animation? Remember when I mentioned how I was barely getting any popularity or views(my most popular animation at the time was only 400 views which I found exciting at the time). Remember when I mentioned how I lost bearcat idol the previous year and I didn't even make it into the finals.

I came up with the idea of the Middle school musical when I was 10 years old. It was only until 7th grade when I started working on it. I had only 3 weeks so I spent my entire break animating. All of my work was worth it when I published it on scratch. At first it had little to no attention as usual, some nice guy really liked it and made a more appealing thumbnail. I think the thumbnail had quite the contribution to its success. The next day I tried putting it on over 200 studios. It was a good technique for making your project more popular but I never tried it before because it would take too long and most of my projects weren't that good. I spent the entire day adding my project to studios.

I then woke up to 320 messages in scratch. It turns out Incognito Orange loved my projects bringing huge popularity. The next day, I thought it died out but what actually happened was that it was in the "What the community is loving"in the front page. This meant I got messages every 10 seconds. By the next week, it reached 11,100 views which was 37x more than my previous most viewed project. Just when the popularity went down a bit, I ended up winning Bearcat idol and became quite popular.

Why don't your characters have pupils?

When I first designed my avatar in fifth grade, I had to choose between four types of cartoon eyes. The most common ones are eyes with pupils(commonly found in almost every cartoon character), only pupils(commonly found in scratch OC's, stickmen or Riddle School characters) or no pupils(found in Annie comics, Pico school characters or country balls). I chose to have no pupils because I thought it would srtand out more.
